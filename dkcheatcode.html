Dunning-Kruger Prompt Cheat Codes

Navigate the confidence curve with targeted prompts

Mount Stupidity

High confidence, low competence. Use these prompts to reveal shallow understanding.

Outline Expander

Category: Breadth

Reveals whether someone understands the full landscape of a topic.

Use Cases: Assess general awareness, scoping exercises
List the major components of [topic]
What are the key disciplines involved in [topic]?

Explainer Ladder

Category: Transferability

Tests if someone can explain the topic at different levels of abstraction.

Use Cases: Teaching, stakeholder alignment
Explain [concept] to a 10-year-old, then to a CEO, then to an expert.

Valley of Despair

Awareness of complexity sets in. Use these prompts to build depth and self-awareness.

Edge Case Trigger

Category: Depth

Tests understanding of exceptions and nuance in a system.

Use Cases: Stress-testing, model evaluation
What are edge cases or failure scenarios in [topic]?
Where does this approach usually break down?

Past Experience Filter

Category: Lived Knowledge

Checks for real-world exposure and lessons learned.

Use Cases: Hiring, retrospectives, mentoring
What did you get wrong when you first worked on [topic]?
Tell me about a time your approach didn’t work—and why.

Slope of Enlightenment

Skill grows. Confidence returns with perspective. Use these prompts to build judgment and reliability.

Trade-off Navigator

Category: Judgment

Encourages critical thinking about competing options.

Use Cases: Architecture choices, project debates
What are the trade-offs between [Option A] and [Option B]?
When is [Method A] better than [Method B], and when is it not?

Self-Critique Request

Category: Reflection

Tests for self-awareness and the ability to spot flaws or biases.

Use Cases: Prompt tuning, performance reviews
What would a skeptic say about this response?
Critique this answer from the perspective of an expert.
